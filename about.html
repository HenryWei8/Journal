About The Journal

    About Us

    The STEM Fellowship Journal (SFJ) is ...

    The SFJ is led by the editor-in-chief and founder of STEM Fellowship, Dr. Sacha Noukhovitch, along with a student editorial board, consisting of dedicated and exceptional high school, undergraduate, and graduate students with strong backgrounds in scientific writing, editing, and communication. SFJ strives to maintain a group of talented and diverse students.

    Aims

    The SFJ publishes STEM research conducted by high school, undergraduate, and graduate students. Our journal is a unique platform to showcase student research from all over the world and serves as the flagship initiative of the Science Communication portfolio at STEM Fellowship.

    Scope

    The SFJ invites original research submissions from any STEM-related field including but not limited to the natural sciences, medicine, engineering, as well as the related social sciences and humanities. We accept submissions that fall under the following categories:

    • Primary research that contributes to the current available scientific evidence and advances knowledge, including interdisciplinary work, negative results, or replication studies.
    • Qualitative research which follows accepted reporting guidelines while also adhering to high standards of ethical and methodological rigour.
    • Viewpoints which present a focused summary and critical analysis of a scientific issue
    • Review articles (e.g., literature, systematic) whose methods ensure the comprehensive and unbiased sampling of existing literature.
